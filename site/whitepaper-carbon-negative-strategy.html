A framework for a carbon-negative mineral strategy

Ocean renewal, lifecycle thinking and permanent storage — evaluating aragonite within carbon-negative roadmaps.

Summary

Aragonite is part of the planet's natural carbon cycle: calcium carbonate is precipitated continuously in warm marine environments, and the Bahamas shallows are one of Earth's most prolific natural depositional zones for oolitic aragonite. AragoCor is building a credible, science-led roadmap for ocean-renewed minerals and lifecycle-aware industrial supply — framed as an evaluated strategy, not a verified claim, pending formal lifecycle analysis (LCA).

The mineral carbon cycle

From atmospheric CO₂ to dissolved bicarbonate to oceanic calcium carbonate precipitation, aragonite sits at the heart of one of Earth's most important long-term carbon storage pathways. Because the same ocean chemistry that formed AragoCor's source deposit continues to operate today, the source itself is renewable at a planetary timescale — distinct from a quarried, finite limestone deposit.

AragoCor frames its position as a carbon-negative strategy, not a verified claim. Emissions accounting that would support a verified carbon-negative designation requires formal, third-party-validated LCA work, which is in progress.

Frequently asked questions

Is AragoCor aragonite verified carbon-negative today?
We frame our position as a carbon-negative strategy. The natural source is renewable and the mineral is part of long-term carbon storage. Verified emissions accounting requires formal LCA — work that is in progress with third-party partners.
How does ocean renewal work?
Calcium carbonate is precipitated continuously in warm marine environments under the right chemistry. The Bahamas shallows are one of the planet's most prolific natural depositional zones for oolitic aragonite.
Are you involved in CCS research?
Yes. We support research partnerships exploring mineral carbonation, enhanced weathering and ocean alkalinity enhancement pathways.
